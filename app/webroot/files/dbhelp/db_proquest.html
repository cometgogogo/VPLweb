How to search Proquest 5000

To access Proquest 5000, select Proquest 5000 from the database list and enter your library card number and PIN.

Basic Searching

Type the topic you are looking to research in the Basic Search bar and click search to retrieve results.

You can use the Database drop down menu to search a specific database, otherwise the default search setting will retrieve results from all proquest databases.

Use the Date Range drop down menu to narrow your search results by a specific date range. You can use one of the options given, or create your own searchable range.

You can limit your search results to retrieve only full text articles, articles from scholarly journals, or both by checking the appropriate box under Limit results to.

Clicking More Search Options will allow you to conduct a more specific search. By clicking this, you can search by journal title or author. You can also search entire articles or strictly abstracts and citations for terms, search by language, document type and publication type. You can also choose to exclude book reviews, dissertations and newspaper articles.

Citing a Proquest article for your bibliography

A link entitled Cite This appears when you click on your search results. Use this link to automatically format your citation for the article in 6 different formats, including APA and MLA.

Searching within magazines, newspapers and journals

Click Publications to search for a specific journal or publication in Proquest 5000. If the publication is available through this database, a range of publication dates will appear beside the publication name. "Full Text" will appear beside the journal link if complete articles from that source are available
